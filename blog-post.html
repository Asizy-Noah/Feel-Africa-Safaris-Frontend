    Serengeti National Park - Home to the World's Remaining Mountain Gorillas

    March 15, 2024
    By Sarah Johnson
    8 min read
    Wildlife

    If gorilla trekking safaris is on your wish list, then Uganda is a must visit destination to see gorillas in Africa on your safari tour, named "Pearl of Africa" by Winston Churchill in his book (My African journey). Uganda is a home to more than half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas, Uganda safaris combines Africa Big five safaris and primate safaris, making Uganda a unique African safari location with many things to do in a relatively small destination.

    Trekking Mountain Gorillas

    Trekking mountain gorillas in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest national park is a major highlight of numerous Uganda tour packages. Most safari itineraries in Uganda are tailored to include gorilla trekking; you can do a luxury gorilla trekking safari, budget gorilla trekking safari, and combined gorilla trekking with wildlife. The experience of coming face-to-face with these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat is truly unforgettable.

    Best Time to Visit

    The best time to visit for gorilla trekking is during the dry seasons: June to August and January to February. During these periods, the trails are more accessible, and visibility is excellent. However, gorillas can be trekked year-round, with each season offering unique advantages and experiences.

    What to Expect

    Gorilla trekking is an adventure that requires physical fitness and determination. The trek through the dense forest can take anywhere from 2 to 8 hours, depending on where the gorillas have been spotted. Once you encounter a family of gorillas, you are allowed to spend one hour observing them from a safe distance of seven meters, a truly magical experience.

    "Seeing mountain gorillas in their natural habitat is one of the most profound wildlife experiences you can have. It changes your perspective on life and nature forever."

    - Dr. Jane Goodall, Primatologist

    Photography Tips

    For photography enthusiasts, bring a camera with good low-light performance, as the forest canopy limits sunlight. A telephoto lens (70-200mm) is ideal for capturing shots from the safe distance required. Always prioritize the gorillas' comfort and safety over getting the perfect shot.

    Practical Information

    Permits are required for gorilla trekking and should be booked well in advance. Physical fitness is important, as the trek involves steep terrain and high altitude. Proper equipment including hiking boots, rain gear, and insect repellent are essential. Most importantly, respect the gorillas' space and follow all guidelines provided by your experienced guide.
